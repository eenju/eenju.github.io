Multi station/function glovebox

Two-dimensional materials and their heterostructures are very sensitive to oxygen and water in the ambient, which can be avoided by the inert gas environment of the glovebox. We have a multi station glovebox, including six station physical glovebox and two station chemical glovebox. The oxygen and water content in the glovebox is limited to less than 1 ppm. Besides, plenty of facilities have been integrated to glovebox for multi functional experiments, including microscope, spin coater, transfer platform, thermal evaporation, etc.

Inert Atmosphere Thermal Evaporation

The thermal evaporation could be used for thin film deposition, including Ni, Cr, Ag, Al, Au, MoO3, etc., to meet various experimental needs, e.g., device electrodes preparation.
In addition, the thermal evaporation have been integrated into the glovebox, which could effectively avoid the influence of water and oxygen adsorption.

Inert Atmosphere Transfer Platform

The abundance of two-dimensional materials enables us to construct multi-functional heterostructures. Transfer platform is a general tool for constructing heterostructures of two-dimensional materials.
The transfer platform is also integrated into the glovebox to avoid sample degradation, water and oxygen adsorption, etc.

Electron Beam Lithography System

The preparation of device electrodes or other patterns in our group is mainly realized by Electron Beam Lithography (EBL).
This electron beam lithography system is an ELS3700 product produced by Elionix Inc. in Japan, which could achieve a minimum linewidth of 100 nm.

Vacuum Cryogenic Probe Station

The CRX-6.5K Vacuum Cryogenic Probe Station produced by Lake Shore Cryotronics Inc. allows us to characterize devices at temperatures as low as 8 K.
In addition, a laser probe arm is reserved in the probe station, enables it to carry out abundant optoelectronic measurements.

Inert Atmosphere Probe Station

We also have an inert atmosphere room temperature probe station integrated in the glovebox, in addition to the Vacuum Cryogenic Probe Station.
It enables us to complete the in situ fabrication and measurement of devices in the glovebox, by cooperating with other equipment.

Cryofree® superconducting magnet system

The transport properties of charge carriers need to be characterized at low temperature and high magnetic field. We have a TeslatronPT Cryofree® superconducting magnet system produced by Oxford Instruments.
It allows us to measure at a magnetic flux density of up to 12 T, and the temperature can be reduced to 1.5 K and 300 mK with HE4 and He3 inserts, respectively.

Multi temperature zone tube furnace

We have two three-temperature zone tube furnaces, two double-temperature zone tube furnaces and two single-temperature zone tube furnaces, could be used for the growth of various materials, device annealing, etc.
