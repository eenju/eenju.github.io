About Us

 Our research group, established in July 2016, is affiliated with the School of Electronic Science and Engineering, Nanjing University. We are devoted to develop sciences and technologies for post-Moore nano-electronics. Our research interests include the synthesis of two-dimensional materials, device physics, and advanced processing and integration techniques.

Recent Progress

>> News

The semi-suspended hysteresis-free field-effect transistor fabricated by doctoral candidate Jiachen Tang holds the potential for exploiting reliable electronics as well as nanofluid and pressure sensors. The research result was published in Applied Physics Letters under the title "A nanogapped hysteresis-free field-effect transistor".
2022.07.11

The low-work function metal ytterbium (Yb) was chosen by doctoral candidate Shihao Ju as contact material for WS2 to lower the schottky barrier. The research result was published in Applied Physics Letters under the title "Electrical contact properties between Yb and few-layer WS2".
2022.06.21

Doctoral candidate Jian Zhou has passed his doctoral defense on May 19, 2022. The defense committee consists of Prof. Yufeng Guo (Vice President of Nanjing University of Posts and Telecommunications), Prof. Zhenhua Ni (Southeast University), Prof. Jinlan Wang (Southeast University), Prof. Xiaomu Wang (Nanjing University), and Prof. Junzhuan Wang (Nanjing University). Associate Professor Songlin Li participated in the defense as doctoral supervisor, and Dr. Yuhao Li served as defense secretary.
2022.05.19

A technique combining lithography-defined contact and clean BN encapsulation has been developed by doctoral candidate Binxi Liang, to improve the overall device quality. The research result was published in ACS Applied Materials & Interfaces under the title "Clean BN-Encapsulated 2D FETs with Lithography-Compatible Contacts".
2022.04.18

Doctoral candidate Jian Zhou used defect engineering to artificially control the diffusion depth of metal Al in two-dimensional TMDC material, and realized the layer by layer digital etching of two-dimensional TMDC material. The research result was published in Nature Communications under the title "Non-invasive digital etching of van der Waals semiconductors".
2022.04.05

Doctoral candidate Shuaixing Li successfully prepared α-MnTe by molten salt assisted chemical vapor deposition, and characterized the crystal quality and magnetic transport characteristics of α-MnTe in detail. The research result was published in Chemistry of Materials under the title "Antiferromagnetic α-MnTe: Molten-Salt-Assisted Chemical Vapor Deposition Growth and Magneto-Transport Properties".
